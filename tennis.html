§ Tennis

All-weather tennis, under the lights.

Four Astroturf® courts, floodlit and code-locked for members, with qualified coaching available for all ages and standards.

§ Play

Social, competitive and coached.

  1. 01

    Four all-weather courts

    Astroturf® surface, floodlit, with meter-controlled lights so members pay only for the time they play.

  2. 02

    Coaching for all ages

    Coaching available with a Level 3 qualified tennis coach — from beginners through to established players looking to sharpen their game.

  3. 03

    League teams

    The club enters teams in the local leagues across the season for members seeking regular competitive play.

  4. 04

    Members-only access

    Courts are code-locked and accessed by members only, keeping play secure and well-managed.

§ Safeguarding

Safeguarding policy statement.

Florence Tennis and Bowling Club acknowledges the duty of care to safeguard and promote the welfare of children and adults at risk. The club is committed to ensuring safeguarding practice reflects statutory responsibilities, government guidance and LTA requirements. A copy of the full policy is available from the club.

The policy recognises that the welfare and interests of children and adults at risk are paramount in all circumstances. It aims to ensure that, regardless of age, ability or disability, gender reassignment, race, religion or belief, sex or sexual orientation, or socio-economic background, all children:

  • Have a positive and enjoyable experience of tennis at the club, in a safe and inclusive environment
  • Are protected from abuse whilst participating in tennis

The club acknowledges that some children — including disabled children and young people, or those from ethnic minority communities — can be particularly vulnerable to abuse, and we accept the responsibility to take reasonable and appropriate steps to ensure their welfare.

As part of our safeguarding policy, the club will:

  • Promote and prioritise the safety and well-being of children and adults at risk
  • Ensure everyone understands their roles and responsibilities, with appropriate learning opportunities to recognise and respond to concerns
  • Encourage a culture where concerns can be raised in confidence and are taken seriously
  • Take appropriate action in the event of any concern, and support those who raise them
  • Maintain confidential, detailed and accurate records, securely stored
  • Prevent the employment or deployment of unsuitable individuals
  • Ensure robust safeguarding arrangements are in operation

Club Welfare Officer: Neil McPherson — 07909 681909

Whistleblowing

Anyone who does not feel comfortable raising a concern with the Club Welfare Officer should contact the LTA Safeguarding Team directly on 020 8487 7000, the Local Authority Designated Officer (LADO), or the NSPCC on 0808 800 5000. Further detail is available in the club's safeguarding policy.

Monitoring

The club's safeguarding policy is reviewed every two years, or sooner if:

  • There are changes in legislation or government guidance
  • Required by the Local Safeguarding Children Board or the LTA
  • Any other significant change or event requires it
